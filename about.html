About Me

Hello, my name is Adryn Gonzales and I am currently a Junior Computer Engineering major attending Texas A&M University. I was born and raised in Von Ormy, Texas, a small town just outside of San Antonio. Some of my hobbies and interests include programming, playing basketball, working out, and lately I have been trying to learn how to play guitar.

I was first introduced to computer programming my Sophomore year of high school in an intro to programming class that I accidently found myself in. To make a long story short, I had to switch classes mid-semester and that was the only class with available seats. Until then, I knew next to nothing about programming and thought to myself that it would just be a ‘throwaway’ course. While we just did projects mainly in educational languages such as Scratch and Jeroo, I fell in love with the process of making something great through lines of code. I instantly knew that this is what I wanted to do for the rest of my life. As my high school did not offer AP Computer Science or anything related, I decided to start learning Python by myself and to start pursuing a career in Computer Engineering.

I chose Computer Engineering rather than Computer Science because while I do really enjoy software development , I have always liked fidgeting with electronics and working on physical devices. I believed that Computer Engineering was a happy medium between two levels of development, hardware, and software. While attending Texas A&M, I have learned so much about the world of Computer Engineering through my coursework, or information from organizations such as IEEE. I now have a better understanding of what my potential career can entail, and what actions I can take that would lead to success not just for me, but for the technological world we are living in today.
